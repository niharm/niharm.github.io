MOSQUITOES IN THE RAIN

Georgia Institute of Technology | Summer 2011

Mosquitoes survive in wet regions, where they can be hit by drops can be 50 times their size. We studied how they survive these collisions using high speed videography, and found that mosquitoes survived by virtue of their small mass and strong exoskeleton.

This project was conducted with Andrew Dickerson, Peter Shankles, and David Hu at Georgia Tech.

We published a paper with PNAS, which can be found here. Here is a video of the project.
