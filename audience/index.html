PERFORMER + AUDIENCE INTERACTION

Princeton Senior Thesis

As part of my Princeton senior thesis, we created a system with the Princeton Laptop Orchestra (PLOrk) by which the audience could interact with performers during a music performance using mobile devices.

We wanted to reconfigure the role of an "audience," by empowering the audience members to influence the piece and to act as performers themselves. Additionally, we aimed to augment the listening experience in a music performance by using mobile devices as speakers.

This system was designed and built with the target of performance in the Spring 2015 Princeton Laptop Orchestra concert. We designed four sections to the piece, testing various relationships between performers and the audience, ultimately resulting in a successful concert performance.
